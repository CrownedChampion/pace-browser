Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026
🛡 Your browsing data stays on your device. We don't sell it, we don't track you, and we never will.

What Data We Collect

Pace Browser does not collect, transmit, or store your personal data on any server. We have no analytics, no telemetry, and no account system. Everything Pace remembers — history, bookmarks, settings, saved passwords — lives only on your computer.

Fast Mode

When Fast Mode is enabled, Pace makes background network requests to websites as you type. These requests originate from your IP and are visible to destination servers. We do not log them. You can disable Fast Mode in Settings → Performance.

Ad Blocking

Pace's built-in ad blocker filters requests against ad and tracker domain lists stored locally. No browsing data is sent to any filtering service.

Password Manager

Pace's optional password manager stores your saved logins encrypted on your device only. Your credentials are protected with industry-standard encryption (AES-256-GCM) derived from a master password that you choose. Your master password is never stored anywhere and is never sent off your device. Because of this, no one — including That1Dev — can recover your saved passwords if you forget your master password. Saved credentials are never transmitted to us or any third party.

Third-Party Extensions & Sidebar Apps

Extensions and sidebar apps operate under their own privacy policies. Pace does not intercept their data. Sidebar apps run in isolated, sandboxed views.

The Website

This website is static and sets no tracking cookies. If you submit the feature-request form, the information you enter is sent by email to the developer (via a third-party form relay) solely to respond to your request. Providing your name and email is optional.

Your Rights & Control

  • View your history any time at pace://history
  • Clear all data from Settings → Clear Browsing Data
  • Manage or delete saved passwords in the password manager
  • Uninstall Pace to remove all local data
